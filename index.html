About this project
An independent research and technology project
BridgeCheck
Independent technology project · Community mental health access
BridgeCheck is an independent technology project focused on improving access to mental health resources through adaptive decision-support.

It was built to answer a simple question: why do so many people who need mental health support never find it? Not because resources don't exist — but because the barriers between people and care are invisible to the systems meant to help them. BridgeLogic™ was designed to make those barriers visible, and route around them.

All resources are verified against public databases and government sources. For feedback, questions, or collaboration inquiries, get in touch.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026 · BridgeCheck
BridgeCheck is designed to be fully anonymous. We do not collect your name, email, location, IP address, or any mental health information you enter into the assessment.

What we collect

BridgeCheck collects nothing that identifies you. The assessment runs entirely in your browser. Your answers are never sent to a server, stored in a database, or linked to any account.

If anonymous aggregate analytics are ever enabled in a future version, only non-identifiable counts will be collected (e.g., "32 people selected 'cost' as a barrier this week"). No session IDs, IP addresses, or response sequences will be stored.

Cookies and tracking

BridgeCheck does not use cookies, tracking pixels, or any third-party analytics tools (such as Google Analytics). There are no ads, no ad networks, and no data brokers.

Third-party resources

BridgeCheck links to external resources (988 Lifeline, NAMI, Open Path Collective, SAMHSA, etc.). Once you leave BridgeCheck and visit those sites, their own privacy policies apply. We encourage you to review those policies independently.

Children

BridgeCheck is intended for users of all ages seeking mental health resource information. We do not knowingly collect any information from anyone.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated date at the top of this page will reflect it. We will never introduce data collection that identifies users without clear disclosure.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Reach out through the project's contact information listed on the home page.

Disclaimer

Last updated: June 2026 · BridgeCheck
BridgeCheck is an informational navigation tool. It is not a medical service, not a licensed clinical platform, and not a substitute for professional mental health care.

Not a diagnosis

BridgeCheck does not diagnose mental health conditions. The assessment questions are designed to identify access barriers, not to evaluate symptoms or clinical need. Nothing in this tool should be interpreted as a clinical assessment, screening, or recommendation to pursue or avoid any particular treatment.

Not emergency care

If you are in a mental health emergency — including thoughts of suicide or self-harm, or if you are in immediate danger — do not use this tool. Call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.

Resource accuracy

BridgeCheck links to publicly available mental health resources verified against government and nonprofit databases. We make reasonable efforts to keep this information current, but:

  • Resource availability, eligibility, and fees can change.
  • Always confirm details directly with each provider before relying on them.
  • BridgeCheck is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or a referral partner of any listed resource.

No professional relationship

Using BridgeCheck does not create a patient-provider relationship, a counselor-client relationship, or any professional relationship of any kind. BridgeCheck is an independent technology project, not a licensed healthcare or counseling service.

Limitation of liability

BridgeCheck and its creators are not liable for any decisions made based on information provided by this tool. Users should exercise independent judgment and consult qualified professionals for medical and mental health decisions.

Intended use

BridgeCheck is intended to help people discover publicly available mental health resources they may not have known about, by surfacing options that address their specific access barriers. It is designed for educational and community benefit.
